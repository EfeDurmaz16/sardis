Conventions

How to read these.

Every claim is sourced
When a canvas names a file, a router, or a migration, it's because that thing exists today in the Sardis monorepo. File paths are literal. If something has drifted, trust the code.
"Why" is load-bearing
Knowing what a module does is cheap — grep finds it. Knowing why it's shaped that way (a past incident, a compliance constraint, a protocol quirk) is the hard part. The canvases spend more words on why than what.
Monokrom on purpose
No gradients, no accent colors fighting for attention, no emoji icons. The in-product dashboard uses grayscale because operators don't want their eyes fighting the UI. These canvases inherit that discipline.
AI-friendly by design
llms.txt (table of contents) and llms-full.txt (full concatenated text) live at the root. Any large-context model can ingest the whole stack and answer specific questions — faster than reading linearly.
