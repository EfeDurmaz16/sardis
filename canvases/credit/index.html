Canvas 23 · Product

Agent Credit — facility access without Sardis becoming the lender.

Facility Gate is Sardis's credit control plane for agents. It lets an agent request temporary purchase authority against a partner-backed facility, but keeps Sardis out of custody, issuing, balance-sheet lending, and collections. Sardis decides whether authority exists; the sponsor or partner remains the economic actor.

New Facility Gate Non-custodial control plane Simulator-first No live card issuance by default
TL;DR Sardis does not hand an agent a general-purpose credit card. The agent submits a facility request; Sardis loads persisted mandate, facility, policy, risk, evidence, revocation, and liability state; then appends an immutable decision packet. Execution is gated through a simulator today and a provider adapter later. Authorization and money movement stay separated.
Boundary

The product is a credit authority firewall.

The architecture deliberately avoids the tempting but dangerous framing: "credit card for agents." A card is only one possible execution rail. The durable primitive is delegated facility authority.

What Sardis controls
Identity binding, mandate scope, policy evaluation, risk scoring, evidence capture, human step-up, authorization, revocation, exception handling, and audit reconstruction. These are control-plane functions, not custody functions.
What Sardis does not become
Sardis is not the lender, issuer of record, stored-value holder, collections system, or repayment obligor. Liquidity and settlement belong to the sponsor, customer-controlled account, or partner facility provider.

Non-custodial line

Where custody stays out of Sardis.

Agent
Delegated actor
Can request authority and attach evidence. Cannot create a facility, expand a limit, or become the repayment obligor.
Sponsor
Economic principal
Owns the mandate and liability. The audit packet names the repayment obligor and loss owner explicitly.
Sardis
Authority control plane
Evaluates, gates, records, revokes, and reconstructs decisions. It does not hold funds or issue credit.
Facility
Partner-backed liquidity
Represents an external or sponsor-backed line. Sardis stores state and version refs, not the balance-sheet obligation.
Adapter
Execution gate
Simulator today; real provider later. No credential is created unless an authorization event already exists.
Partner
Rail / issuing / settlement
Moves money or provides credit under its own contract. Webhooks mirror external state back into append-only events.
Decision packet

Every approval is reconstructable.

The audit surface is not a screenshot of current state. It is a versioned packet that says exactly what Sardis knew and which authority it relied on when the decision happened.

Mandate version
Explicit facility draw authority, agent binding, merchant/category scope, draw caps, approval threshold.
Facility version
Provider, sponsor, status, limit payload, allowed categories, blocked merchants, repayment mirror if present.
Policy and risk versions
Deterministic policy snapshot, risk model version, feature snapshot, reason codes. No hidden model magic.
Evidence considered
Only evidence attached before decision time is considered. Later evidence requires a new decision path.
Liability assignment
Repayment obligor, sponsor, loss owner, and facility provider are first-class fields, not buried in metadata.
Event hash chain
Requests, approvals, executions, exceptions, revocations, webhooks, and exports sit on append-only events.
