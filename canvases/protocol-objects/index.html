Canvas 20 · Protocol

Protocol Objects — the 10 signed artifacts.

Sardis v1.0 defines 10 protocol objects that chain together from delegation through settlement, dispute resolution, and fulfillment. Every object is cryptographically signed, immutable once issued, and references its predecessors. Together they form a complete audit chain — no trust in Sardis required.

TL;DR A reusable funding credential — card, wallet, bank credential, or API key — is the wrong primitive for the agent economy. The right primitive is the One-Time Payment Object: a signed, short-lived, context-bound instrument backed by a UTXO-style funding commitment, tied to a verified agent and principal, bounded by policy, and auditable with a complete cryptographic trail.
Object chain

From delegation to fulfillment in 10 objects.

1 · Agent Certificate

Identity
Identifies the agent and binds it to an issuer and a principal context. Without cryptographic agent identity, you don't have agent payments — you have bot payments. TAP-compatible so agents can prove identity to any Visa-network merchant.
agent_id · issuer_id · principal_id · agent_public_key (Ed25519 or P-256 JWK)
attestation_type (self_signed | issuer_attested | network_verified)
trust_tier (0–3) · capabilities (browse, pay, subscribe, delegate) · expires_at · signature
Trust tiers: 0 = unverified ($10 max tx, email only) · 1 = issuer-attested ($500, KYC-lite) · 2 = network-verified ($10K, full KYC + TAP) · 3 = enterprise-grade (policy-defined, full IAM + continuous monitoring).

2 · Spending Mandate

Authorization
The signed statement of allowed authority from the principal — a programmable, cryptographically signed power of attorney for spending. Heart of the system. Expressible as an AP2 Verifiable Digital Credential, extended with agent-native fields (in_flight_limit, fx_policy, UTXO cell strategy).
mandate_id · principal_id · agent_id · merchant_scope · category_scope
max_amount_per_tx · max_amount_per_period · period_type (daily | weekly | monthly)
currency_scope · rail_scope · approval_threshold · retry_limit
in_flight_limit · fx_policy · valid_from · expires_at · policy_hash
revocation_handle · nonce · signature (Ed25519/P-256)
Security — in_flight_limit: prevents the T4 "shadow lock" attack where a malfunctioning agent freezes all funds by requesting objects without presenting them. Agent must settle or cancel before minting new objects beyond the limit.

3 · Funding Commitment

Reserve
Reserve-backed container bound to a specific mandate. Guarantees funds are available before any payment object is minted. In v0 this was a monolithic lock against a vault balance — v1 replaces it with a UTXO model to allow concurrent spending without race conditions.
commitment_id · mandate_id
funding_source_type (custodial_fiat | smart_wallet | card | bank | stablecoin | credit_line)
funding_source_ref · total_committed · cell_strategy (pre_split | on_demand | hybrid)
default_cell_denomination · commitment_expiry · settlement_preferences
fallback_options · refund_destination · issuer_signature
Security — refund_destination: must always point to a permanent Principal Master Treasury, never a temp vault or cell. Prevents the T6 "refund-to-closed-vault" failure where disputes resolve months after the original transaction and the funding container is gone.

4 · FundingCell (UTXO)

Concurrency
A discrete, atomic unit of spendable value within a Funding Commitment. Inspired by Bitcoin's UTXO model: each cell has exactly one owner at any time; concurrent payments claim different cells; double-spending is structurally impossible without distributed consensus.
cell_id (primary key for locking) · commitment_id · mandate_id
denomination (fixed) · state (AVAILABLE | CLAIMED | SPENT | RETURNED | EXPIRED | SPLIT | MERGED)
claimed_by · claimed_at · payment_object_id · parent_cell_id · vault_ref
created_at · expires_at · nonce · issuer_signature
Why it beats a global counter: no distributed locking. PostgreSQL FOR UPDATE SKIP LOCKED ensures concurrent transactions never block each other. Cells split on-demand (an agent needing $7 from a $10 cell produces $7 CLAIMED + $3 AVAILABLE), merge in background to prevent fragmentation. Horizontally scalable.

5 · One-Time Payment Object

Execution
The single-use executable payment artifact — what the agent presents to the merchant. Derived from the mandate + funding commitment cell(s). Every payment creates a fresh object; no credential is ever reused. This is the core innovation of the protocol.
payment_object_id · mandate_id · commitment_id · cell_ids (UTXO inputs)
merchant_id · session_hash (sha256: merchant_id | cart_id | timestamp)
exact_amount · source_currency · target_currency · nonce · one_time_use (true)
expires_at (minutes–hours) · privacy_mode (transparent | hybrid | full_zk)
zk_proof (if non-transparent) · escrow_required · escrow_profile · dispute_profile
signature_chain [principal_sig, issuer_sig, agent_sig]
Security — session_hash: binds payment to a specific merchant session/cart. Prevents the T3 relay/MITM attack where an intermediary intercepts a valid payment and replays it in a different shopping context.

6 · Settlement Receipt

Proof
Canonical proof of what actually happened. Closes the audit chain. v1 adds fulfillment_status to track whether the agent acknowledged receipt of the purchased service/good, plus FX fields for cross-currency transactions.
receipt_id · payment_object_id · settlement_rail · settlement_tx_ref
settled_amount · fees · fx_quote_id · actual_fx_rate · fx_fee
timestamp · status (settled | partial_settled | failed | reversed)
fulfillment_status (pending_ack | acknowledged | dispute_auto_triggered)
merchant_signature · network_signature
fulfillment_status (new in v1): if not acknowledged within escrow timeout, dispute_auto_triggered fires automatically. Closes the T11 "paid but not delivered" gap — settlement succeeds but merchant fails to provide the service.

7 · EscrowHold

Trust
For transactions above the escrow threshold, funds land in escrow before the merchant receives them. Merchant sees cryptographic proof of locked funds and delivers the service; agent confirms receipt (or timelock expires); then escrow releases. If dispute: arbitration decides.
escrow_id · payment_object_id · held_amount
held_at (issuer_vault | smart_contract) · contract_address · beneficiary
release_condition (AGENT_CONFIRM | TIMELOCK | ORACLE) · timeout_duration · auto_release_at
state (HELD | CONFIRMING | AUTO_RELEASING | RELEASED | DISPUTING | ARBITRATING | RESOLVED_*)
dispute_window · evidence_deadline · arbitration_endpoint · issuer_signature
Default timeouts by transaction type: API/SaaS = 1h escrow, 24h dispute, 3-day evidence · Software license = 24h / 7d / 5d · Physical goods = 14d / 30d / 7d · B2B services = 30d / 60d / 14d. Timeouts match delivery verification complexity.

8 · FXQuote

Cross-currency
Records the cross-currency conversion applied during settlement. Linked from the Settlement Receipt. Time-bounded quote with explicit slippage tracking.
fx_quote_id · source_currency · target_currency
quoted_rate · quoted_at · quote_valid_until (default 30s)
source_amount · target_amount · fx_fee · total_source_debit
slippage_actual_bps · provider · provider_tx_ref
Matched against the mandate's fx_policy at verification time. If slippage_actual_bps exceeds max_slippage_bps or the provider isn't in preferred_fx_providers, the payment fails before settlement.

9 · Subscription

Recurring
Recurring charge intents + billing cycles + dunning rules. Not a single mandate but a parent that emits child one-time payment objects on schedule. Covers SaaS subscriptions, usage-based billing, and agent-mediated BNPL (credit-backed mandates).
subscription_id · parent_mandate_id · merchant_id
charge_intent · billing_cycle (ISO 8601 duration) · dunning_policy
amendment_protocol (price_creep_protection)
grace_period · usage_counter · countersignature (for usage-based)
Price-creep protection: if the merchant issues an amendment that exceeds mandate.amendment_tolerance_bps, the subscription pauses pending principal re-signature. Prevents silent subscription inflation that consumer credit cards suffer from.

10 · Mandate Tree

Delegation
Hierarchical delegation. CFO creates a master mandate ($500K/quarter); VP Engineering creates a child mandate ($100K/quarter) under it; team lead creates a grandchild ($20K/month). Each level further constrains — never expands — the parent's bounds. Maps to how enterprise budgets actually work.
tree_root_mandate_id · parent_mandate_id · depth · path
inherited_constraints (merchant_scope ∩, category_scope ∩, limits ≤ parent)
delegation_signature (parent signs child) · revocation_cascade
Enforcement rule: child mandate constraints MUST be a strict subset of parent. Issuer rejects creation if any dimension expands. Revocation cascades down the tree — revoking a parent invalidates all descendants.
Chain

How they reference each other.

Each object is signed and references its predecessors, forming a chain that any party can verify independently. Canvas 21 · 22-State Machine shows how these objects transition through states over time.

  SM --> FC[Funding Commitment]
  FC --> FCell[FundingCell UTXO]
  SM --> MT[Mandate Tree children]
  SM -. recurring .-> SUB[Subscription]
  FCell --> POO[One-Time Payment Object]
  SM --> POO
  POO --> EH[EscrowHold optional]
  POO --> SR[Settlement Receipt]
  SR --> FXQ[FXQuote if cross-currency]
  EH --> SR
