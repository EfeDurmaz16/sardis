# Code of Conduct

Sardis contributors are expected to keep discussion technical, respectful, and focused on making the project safer and more useful.

## Standards

- Be direct and constructive.
- Assume good faith, but anchor technical claims in evidence.
- Respect security-sensitive boundaries.
- Do not post secrets, credentials, customer data, private sales material, or vulnerability details in public issues.
- Do not pressure maintainers to merge changes that weaken payment, wallet, policy, compliance, or evidence safeguards.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Publicly disclosing security vulnerabilities before coordinated disclosure.
- Adding malicious code, credential exfiltration, hidden telemetry, or unsafe payment behavior.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, close, or remove issues, PRs, comments, or contributions that violate this code. Security reports should follow `SECURITY.md`.
