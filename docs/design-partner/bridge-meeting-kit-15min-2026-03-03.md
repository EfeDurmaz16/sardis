# Bridge Meeting Kit (15 min, Talk-Only, No Demo)

Date: 2026-03-03  
Owner: Efe (Founder, Sardis)  
Format: conversation only, no live demo

## 1) Meeting objective

Get explicit agreement on:
1. Production onboarding path and timeline
2. Required compliance/operational prerequisites
3. Live-lane scope for first design-partner cohort
4. Named owners for technical + partnership tracks

## 2) Opening script

`Thanks for joining. I’ll keep this very concrete. No live demo today. The goal is to lock production onboarding steps, timeline, and owners so we can run a controlled live lane for design partners.`

## 3) 15-minute agenda with exact talking lines

## 0:00-2:00 - Context

Use these lines:
- `Sardis is the policy and control plane for agent payments.`
- `Bridge is strategic for fiat/stablecoin movement in our production architecture.`
- `We are in design-partner rollout mode, not broad public scale.`

## 2:00-6:00 - What we need from Bridge

Use these lines:
- `We need a concrete production enablement checklist and target date.`
- `We need clarity on limits, settlement states, and operational runbooks.`
- `We need clear webhook/reconciliation expectations for deterministic operations.`
- `We need ownership mapping for compliance boundaries and incident escalation.`

## 6:00-10:00 - Integration scope (no demo)

Use these lines:
- `Our core stance is fail-closed policy enforcement before execution.`
- `We want to align on lifecycle states end-to-end: initiation, pending, settled, failed, reversed.`
- `We also want to align on idempotency, retries, replay handling, and webhook signatures.`
- `For us, reliability and auditability are more important than broad feature expansion in phase one.`

## 10:00-13:00 - Rollout proposal

Use these lines:
- `Phase 1 is controlled design-partner lane with limited volume and explicit risk guardrails.`
- `Phase 2 expands corridors and throughput after reliability metrics pass.`
- `We can share weekly scorecards for success rate, reconciliation lag, and incident response quality.`

## 13:00-15:00 - Direct close and ask

Use these lines:
- `To move forward, I need three decisions now.`
- `First: named technical owner and partner owner.`
- `Second: production onboarding checklist with hard prerequisites.`
- `Third: target date for first controlled live transactions.`

## 4) If they ask “What is your current stage?”

Use these lines:
- `We have the integration architecture and control surfaces in place, and we are now locking production partner lanes.`
- `Today is about turning that readiness into a concrete joint execution plan.`

## 5) Final sentence to end meeting

`If we align on owner, checklist, and target date today, I will send a written rollout plan and success metrics sheet in 24 hours.`
