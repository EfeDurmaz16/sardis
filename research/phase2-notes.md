# Phase 2 Notes

## Scoring rubric

- `fit_score`: How directly Sardis maps to a live or near-term workflow at the company.
- `confidence_score`: How much public evidence exists that the workflow is active now.
- `reachability_score`: How realistic it is to reach a relevant buyer or champion in the next 30 to 60 days.
- `personalization_richness_score`: How much public context exists for a strong custom outbound angle.
- `pilot_likelihood_score`: How plausible a design-partner or pilot conversation is in the near term.

## Track logic

- `Agent platforms / agent builders`: Companies building agent frameworks, agent operating systems, workflow agents, browser agents, or agent marketplaces.
- `Internal enterprise AI and automation teams`: Companies likely to use Sardis inside their own finance, ops, developer, or procurement automation stack.
- `Fintech / commerce / payment design partners`: Companies that could use Sardis as a control layer over cards, wallets, treasury, checkout, or stablecoin rails.
- `Commerce / marketplace / travel / local commerce platforms`: Companies where agentic checkout, ordering, booking, or service procurement is a concrete workflow.

## Caveats

- Tier 1 names generally have stronger public signal and clearer workflow fit.
- Tier 2 names are strong but more dependent on messaging discipline and contact selection.
- Tier 3 names are still defensible, but several are best treated as testing volume rather than immediate high-conviction pilots.
- Reachability drops sharply for mega-cap platforms even when fit is high.
