# Phase 2 Target Universe

Scoring rubric:
- Fit: structural match to Sardis's wedge, which is policy-controlled agent spend, approvals, auditability, compliance, and payment workflow insertion.
- Confidence: strength of current public evidence that the company is investing in the relevant workflow.
- Reachability: practical likelihood of reaching the right team or operator with founder-led outbound.
- Personalization richness: amount of public context available for a custom email.
- Pilot likelihood: practical chance of a near-term design partner or pilot conversation.

Research basis:
- Current public product launches, official blogs, press releases, docs, and visible workflow posture as of March 10, 2026.
- This list is intentionally segmented by ICP track and tier rather than ranked as a flat scrape.
- Tier 1 contains the top 25 highest-priority companies overall.
- Tier 2 contains the next 35 strong-fit companies.
- Tier 3 contains the final 40 broader but still defensible companies.

## Selection Notes

- Highest-priority companies are not just the biggest logos. Tier 1 weights fit, timing, and plausible pilot motion more heavily than prestige.
- Several large incumbents remain in Tier 2 or Tier 3 despite strategic importance because reachability and pilot speed are weaker.
- Procurement and spend platforms score unusually well because Sardis's value is easiest to explain where approvals, policy, auditability, and execution already sit in one workflow.
- Agent platforms score best where they are shifting from assistance into action-taking workflows.
- Internal enterprise AI teams are included only when there is visible AI or automation posture plus a believable route into payment-adjacent operations.
